Q.2.(A) — Extract: The Traveller and His Bag  |  Complete the activities 12 Marks
A-1) Global Understanding [2 Marks]
Complete the sentence by choosing two correct alternatives: "The souvenirs [represented] personal freedom because…"

The two correct alternatives are (1) and (4):

  1. (1) They symbolized personal freedom — When the man finally built a statue of all his objects and left them behind, he experienced true personal freedom from his burdensome past.
  2. (4) There were bitter memories attached to them — Each souvenir was collected during a misfortune the traveller had faced, so they carried painful memories that weighed him down.
A-2) Complex Factual [2 Marks]
Describe the two scenes enacted by the actor. (about 50 words)

The actor enacted two scenes. In the first scene, he imitated the burdened man — picking things up, working harder and harder until he collapsed exhausted. In the second scene, after the traveller recognised himself and began to weep, the actor dramatically enacted the story connected to each souvenir from the bag in a theatrical fashion.

A-3) Inference / Interpretation / Analysis [2 Marks]
Guess the advice given through the story. (about 50 words)

The story advises us that we should not burden ourselves with the painful memories and regrets of the past. Just as the traveller collected souvenirs from every misfortune and carried them silently, we too tend to hold on to our sorrows. True personal freedom lies in letting go of these emotional burdens and moving forward with a light heart.

A-4) Personal Response [2 Marks]
Explain in about 50 words: "Burden of life becomes a real burden when not shared."

This quote means that the difficulties and hardships of life feel much heavier when we face them alone. When we share our problems with others, the weight becomes lighter. Just as the traveller's bag grew heavier as he silently collected his sorrows, sharing our pain with loved ones provides relief, emotional strength, and a sense of belonging.

(ii) He looked at the bag and started crying. (Rewrite using 'No sooner….than')

No sooner did he look at the bag than he started crying.

Note: Both actions are simultaneous here — simple past 'did…look' is used, not past perfect.

Q.2.(B) — Summary Writing 3 Marks
Write a summary with a suitable title. Hints: heavy bag, street actor, personal freedom, message.

Title: The Burden of Memories


A man set out on a long journey carrying a heavy bag. Whenever he faced a misfortune, he placed a souvenir into his bag as a reminder, making it heavier with every passing mile. At a crossroad, he came across a group of street actors. One actor imitated the burdened man, picking things up until he fell exhausted. When the traveller recognised himself, he began to weep. The actor then enacted the story of each souvenir. Finally, the actor suggested the man build a statue of all his objects as a monument. The man did so, and upon leaving it behind, he experienced personal freedom for the first time. The message of the story is that we must let go of the painful memories of the past to achieve true freedom and peace in life.

Q.3.(A) — Poem: "Between Nose and Eyes"  |  Complete the activities 10 Marks
A-1) Global Understanding [2 Marks]

(1) True or False (correct if false):
"The Ear was appointed as a judge."

TRUE. — "Chief Baron Ear sat to balance the laws, so famed for his talent in nicely discerning" confirms Ear was the judge.


(2) Expressions from the extract arguing in favour of the Nose:

  • "The Nose has had spectacles always in wear, which amounts to possession time out of mind."
  • "The spectacles were made with a straddle as wide as the ridge of the Nose is; in short, designed to sit close to it, just like a saddle."
  • "The spectacles plainly were made for the Nose, and the Nose was as plainly intended for them."
A-2) Inferences / Interpretation / Analysis [2 Marks]

(1) Meaning of phrases:

  • (a) Unhappily wrong: To cause unpleasant trouble or discomfort; to put someone in a state of conflict.
  • (b) Time out of mind: Since time immemorial; from a time so long ago that no one can remember.

(2) For what reason were Ear and Tongue given the duties?

Ear was appointed as judge because he was "famed for his talent in nicely discerning" — he could listen carefully and make fair judgements.

Tongue was appointed as lawyer because she argued "with great deal of skill, and a wig full of learning" — she was eloquent, knowledgeable and skilled in argumentation.

A-3) Personal Response [2 Marks]
Justify the arguments of the Tongue in the extract.

The Tongue argued brilliantly on behalf of both sides. Her arguments for the Nose were based on (i) long possession — "The Nose has had spectacles always in wear, which amounts to possession time out of mind" — and (ii) physical design — the spectacles were shaped to sit on the Nose like a saddle. Shifting sides, she then argued for the Eyes — that without the Eyes, no one would need spectacles at all. Both sets of arguments are logically sound, proving that the Tongue is an extremely skilled and witty debater capable of arguing either side of a case convincingly.

A-4) Character Analysis [2 Marks]
The question asks to 'comment on the characters in a sentence or phrase' — this is a Character Analysis question, not Poetic Devices. Label corrected.
Comment on the characters: (a) Nose   (b) Eyes
  • (a) Nose: The Nose is portrayed as the rightful and long-standing claimant of the spectacles — physically designed to hold them and in possession of them since time immemorial.
  • (b) Eyes: The Eyes are the challengers in this dispute — they argue that since they are the organ that actually uses spectacles for vision, the spectacles rightfully belong to them.

Q.3.(B) — Poetic Appreciation of the given poem extract 4 Marks
Write appreciation as per: (1) Theme (2) Language (3) Message (4) My Opinion
(1) Theme

The poem presents a humorous mock legal dispute between the Nose and the Eyes over ownership of spectacles. The theme explores how clever arguments can be constructed for either side of any dispute, and how the most persuasive speaker often prevails over simple truth.

(2) Language

The language is witty, playful, and formal. The poet uses extensive personification, giving human roles to body parts — Nose and Eyes as litigants, Ear as judge, Tongue as lawyer. The tone mimics courtroom formality in a comic setting. The regular rhyme scheme gives the poem a musical, storytelling quality.

(3) Message

The poem conveys that every dispute has two valid sides and that clever arguments can be constructed for either party. It also satirises how legal battles are sometimes won not by truth alone but by the most skilled and persuasive speaker, and that judgements may still leave both sides unsatisfied.

(4) My Opinion

I find this poem highly creative and thought-provoking. The idea of using body parts as courtroom characters is both original and entertaining. It not only amuses the reader but also encourages critical thinking about argument and judgement in everyday life. It is a memorable poem that beautifully blends humour with wisdom.

Q.5(B) — Answer in about 50 words [4 Marks]

(1) Discuss the theme of the play.

The play An Enemy of the People by Henrik Ibsen has the central theme of truth versus social and political pressure. Dr. Stockman discovers that the town's baths are contaminated but faces fierce opposition from authorities who prioritise economic interests over public health. The play powerfully explores moral courage, individual integrity, and the conflict between personal conscience and a corrupt majority.

(2) Write the character sketch of Dr. Stockman.

Dr. Thomas Stockman is the protagonist of Ibsen's play. He is an idealistic, honest and morally courageous man who believes firmly in truth above all else. As Medical Officer of the baths, he discovers water contamination and bravely fights to expose it despite opposition from his brother (the Mayor) and townspeople. He symbolises unwavering integrity and moral courage against a self-interested, corrupt society.

Q.5(C) — Grammar: Do as directed [4 Marks]

(1) Further advance was stalled due to accurate fire by the enemy. (Change the voice)

The enemy's accurate fire stalled the further advance.
Passive → Active: 'by the enemy' becomes the subject. The agent 'the enemy' now performs the action 'stalled'.

(2) I spent my high school years in Mumbai. (Choose the correct past perfect continuous tense form)

(a) I had been spending my high school years in Mumbai.

Reason: Past Perfect Continuous = had been + V-ing, indicating a continuous action before a point in the past.
