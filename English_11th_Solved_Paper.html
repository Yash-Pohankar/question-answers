Q.2.(B) — Summary Writing 3 Marks
Write a summary of the above extract with a suitable title. (Hints: heavy bag, street actor, personal freedom, message)

Title: The Burden of Memories


A man was on a long journey carrying a heavy bag. Whenever he experienced a misfortune, he had a custom of placing a souvenir into his bag as a reminder. As a result, the bag grew heavier and heavier with every passing mile. One day, he arrived at a crossroad where he saw a group of street actors improvising a play. One actor imitated the burdened man, dramatically showing how the weight crushed him until he fell exhausted. When the traveller recognized himself in the performance, he began to cry. The actor then enacted the story of each souvenir. Finally, the actor suggested the traveller build a monument of all his objects. The man did so, and upon leaving the statue behind, he felt a sense of personal freedom for the first time. The message of the story is that we must let go of the painful memories of the past to achieve true freedom and peace in life.

Q.3.(A) — Extract from "Between Nose and Eyes" — Complete the activities 10 Marks
A-1) Global Understanding [2 Marks]

(1) State whether True or False; correct if false:

"The Ear was appointed as a judge."

TRUE. Chief Baron Ear sat to balance the laws, famed for his talent in nicely discerning — meaning Ear was appointed as the judge in this contest.


(2) The Tongue justifies the possession of spectacles on behalf of the Nose. Pick up the expressions from the extract that argue in favour of the Nose.

  • "The Nose has had spectacles always in wear, Which amounts to possession time out of mind."
  • "The spectacles were made with a straddle As wide as the ridge of the Nose is; in short, Designed to sit close to it, just like a saddle."
  • "The spectacles plainly were made for the Nose, And the Nose was as plainly intended for them."
A-2) Inferences / Interpretation / Analysis [2 Marks]

(1) State what you understood by the given phrases:

  • (a) Unhappily wrong — To cause someone unpleasant trouble or set someone in a position of conflict and discomfort.
  • (b) Time out of mind — Since time immemorial; from a time so far back that it cannot be remembered.

(2) For what reason were Ear and Tongue given the duties?

Ear was given the duty of a judge because he was "famed for his talent in nicely discerning" — meaning he had the ability to carefully listen and make fair judgements.

Tongue was given the duty of a lawyer because she argued "with great deal of skill, and a wig full of learning" — meaning she was eloquent, knowledgeable, and skilled in argumentation.

A-3) Personal Response [2 Marks]
Justify the arguments of the Tongue in the extract.

The Tongue argued brilliantly in favour of the Nose. Her first argument was based on long possession — the Nose has always had the spectacles, which amounts to an undeniable claim. Her second argument was based on physical design — the spectacles were made with a straddle as wide as the ridge of the Nose, perfectly fitted like a saddle. These arguments are logically sound because they are supported both by tradition and physical evidence. Even the second half of the poem, where the Tongue argues for the Eyes, shows her skill; but the original arguments for the Nose remain the stronger and more convincing ones.

A-4) Poetic Devices [2 Marks]
Comment on the following characters depicted in the extract, in a sentence or a phrase. (a) Nose   (b) Eyes
  • (a) Nose: The Nose is portrayed as the rightful and long-standing claimant of the spectacles, supported by the fact that they were physically designed to rest upon it.
  • (b) Eyes: The Eyes are depicted as the challenging claimants who dispute the Nose's ownership of the spectacles, arguing that since they are the ones who use spectacles to see, the spectacles should belong to them.

Q.3.(B) — Poetic Appreciation of the given poem extract 4 Marks
Read the poem and write appreciation as per the given points: (1) Theme (2) Language (3) Message (4) My Opinion
(1) Theme

The poem presents a humorous legal dispute between the Nose and the Eyes over the right to own and wear spectacles. It satirically mirrors real-life arguments and courtroom debates where both sides use logic and clever rhetoric to justify their claims.

(2) Language

The language of the poem is witty, playful, and formal. The poet makes extensive use of personification, giving human qualities to body parts like the Nose, Eyes, Ear, and Tongue. The tone mimics a courtroom drama, with formal legal language used in a comical context. The rhyme scheme is regular and adds a musical quality to the poem.

(3) Message

The poem conveys the message that every dispute has two sides, and clever arguments can be constructed for either party. It also satirizes the way legal battles are often won not by truth alone but by the most persuasive speaker. Ultimately, even the wisest judge makes decisions that may not fully satisfy everyone.

(4) My Opinion

I find this poem extremely entertaining and thought-provoking. The poet's creativity in using body parts as characters in a courtroom drama is both original and amusing. The poem not only makes us laugh but also subtly encourages us to think critically about arguments and judgements in everyday life. It is a memorable poem that blends humour with wisdom.

(1) Discuss the theme of the play.

The play An Enemy of the People by Henrik Ibsen has the central theme of truth versus social pressure. Dr. Stockman discovers that the town's baths are contaminated but faces resistance from the authorities and townspeople who prioritize economic interests over public health. The play powerfully explores moral courage, individual integrity, and the conflict between personal conscience and a corrupt majority.

(2) Write the character sketch of Dr. Stockman.

Dr. Thomas Stockman is the protagonist of Ibsen's play. He is an idealistic, honest, and courageous man who firmly believes in truth above all else. As the Medical Officer of the baths, he discovers water contamination and bravely fights to expose it despite opposition from his brother, the Mayor, and the townspeople. He represents the spirit of moral courage and unwavering integrity in the face of a corrupt and self-interested society.
